About

Homelessness is something that could happen to anybody. It could happen to you, your friends, your family. Chances are, you know someone who has been either homeless or on the cusp of being homeless. While it is an unfortunate situation that can impact anyone, it tends to disproportionately impact those who belong to a minority. This could be sexual orientation, gender identity, race, religion, nationality, mental/physical ability, or amount of education.

Race and homelessness

This is a graphic percentage makeup of the different races that are homeless. Even though black people make up only 13% of the population in America, they make up 40% of the homelessness population. This pattern repeats for American Indian/Alaska natives, native Hawaiians, and Pacific Islanders. They disproportionately make up a greater percentage of the homeless population compared to the US as a whole. The hispanic population in the US is approximately equal to the hispanic homeless population. This is all happening while white and Asian populations are significantly not as impacted by homelessness. Learn more at https://endhomelessness.org/resource/racial-inequalities-homelessness-numbers/.

Gender and homelessness

Gender symbols


Gender also impacts the likelihood of ending up homeless. As of recent surveys taken in 2018 the National Alliance to End Homelessness determined that there are 210,284 homeless men compared to 106,119 homeless women. That is almost a ratio of 1:3 for homeless women compared to homeless men. Percentage wise, 70% of the homeless population is male, 29% of the homeless population is female, and 1% is transgender.
